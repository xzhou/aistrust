Professional Organizations:

  • American Association of Nurse Anesthetists home page.
  • The American Society of Anesthesiologists home page.
  • Canadian Anaesthetists' Society - CAS' mission is to dedicate itself to the advancement of the medical practice of anaesthesia through academic endeavour and excellence in patient care.
  • New York State Society of Anesthesiologists - includes information about the Postgraduate Assembly in Anesthesiology.
  • Society for Computing and Technology in Anaesthesia - SCATA is a British society originally formed in 1987 as The Computing in Anaesthesia Society, to provide a forum for those interested in any application of computing in anaesthesia. The interests of the members range from drug distribution modeling to the logic, design and reliability of monitors and the anaesthesia work station. Because of these wider interests, the name of the society was changed to include technology.
  • Society for Pediatric Anesthesia - The purpose of the Society for Pediatric Anesthesia is to foster quality anesthetic care for children. This shall be accomplished by:   1.Assembling in one group anesthesiologists who practice and have a strong interest in pediatric anesthesia. 2.Advancing the study of pediatric anesthesia and contributing to its growth and influence. 3.Encouraging research and scientific progress in pediatric anesthesia. 4.Serving as a forum for discussion of issues (scientific and political) of importance to pediatric anesthesia. 5.Supporting the goals of the American Society of Anesthesiologists and the American Academy of Pediatrics.
  • SAMBA (Society for Ambulatory Anesthesia) - an organization of anesthesiologists, health care professionals and individuals from industry, interested in improving the care of patients undergoing office-based, free-standing, and hospital-based ambulatory surgery.
  • The Australian Society of Anaesthetists
  • National Center for Emergency Medicine Informatics:  The greatest advances in medicine over the next two decades will result from the application of the tools and principles of informatics to the problems of clinical medicine. New developments in informatics will drive advances in clinical care, administration, research, and education.
  • The American Medical Association home page.
  • Healthcare Information and Management Systems Society (HIMSS), Chicago, IL, founded in 1961, is a not-for-profit organization dedicated to promoting a better understanding of health care information and management systems. HIMSS represents over 6,000 health care professionals in four professional areas: Clinical systems, Information systems, Management engineering and Telecommunications.
  • American Medical Informatics Association (AMIA) is the premier association in the United States dedicated to the development and application of medical informatics in the support of patient care, teaching, research, and health care administration. AMIA's mission is to advance the public interest through charitable, scientific, literary, and educational activities.
